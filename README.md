# India-CPI-Inflation-Case-Study
## Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze India's CPI (Consumer Price Index) inflation trends using data from the Government of India. The study aims to identify the major contributors to inflation, examine year-over-year inflation patterns, analyze food inflation trends, assess the impact of COVID-19 on consumer prices, and evaluate the relationship between imported oil prices and inflation. Through data analysis and visualization in Excel, the project provides insights into the key factors influencing inflation in India.
## Dataset used
<a href="https://github.com/Deepak-NEGII/India-CPI-Inflation-Case-Study/blob/main/CPI_Inflation_case_study.xlsx"></a>
## Questions
- Which broad category contributes the most to overall CPI inflation in India?
- How have Year-over-Year (YoY) inflation rates changed from 2017 onwards, and were there any significant periods of high inflation?
- What were the trends in food inflation during the 12 months ending May 2023, and which food categories contributed the most to inflation?
- How did the COVID-19 pandemic affect inflation rates in India, particularly in categories such as Food, Health, Fuel, and Transport?
- What is the relationship between imported crude oil prices and CPI inflation between 2021 and 2023, and which CPI category is most affected by oil price fluctuations?
## Process
- Collected CPI inflation data from the Government of India dataset and imported crude oil price data for 2021–2023.
- Performed data cleaning by identifying missing values and imputing missing observations using interpolation where appropriate.
- Categorized CPI components into broader groups such as Food, Health, Fuel & Light, and Transport & Communication for analysis.
- Analyzed category-wise contribution to identify the major drivers of inflation.
- Calculated Year-over-Year (YoY) inflation rates and examined inflation trends from 2017 onwards.
- Conducted a detailed food inflation analysis for the 12 months ending May 2023, including month-on-month changes and category-level contributions.
- Assessed the impact of COVID-19 by comparing inflation trends before and after March 2020.
-Integrated imported crude oil price data with CPI data and performed correlation analysis to study the relationship between oil prices and inflation.
-Identified the CPI category most affected by oil price fluctuations using correlation analysis.
- Created charts, tables, and visualizations in Excel to summarize findings and derive insights.
## Project Insights
- Food was identified as the largest contributor to overall CPI inflation in India.
- Inflation remained relatively stable before 2020 but increased significantly during 2020–2022.
- The highest YoY inflation was observed in 2022, reaching approximately 7%.
- Food inflation showed noticeable fluctuations during the 12 months ending May 2023.
- Spices recorded the highest increase within the food category, while Oils & Fats showed the largest decline.
- COVID-19 had a significant impact on inflation, particularly in Food, Health, Fuel, and Transport categories.
- Imported crude oil prices showed a moderate positive relationship with overall CPI inflation (r = 0.55).
- Transport & Communication was the category most affected by oil price fluctuations, with the highest correlation (r = 0.667).
## Conclusion
This project analyzed CPI inflation trends in India and identified the key factors influencing inflation. Food emerged as the major contributor to inflation, while inflation levels increased significantly during and after the COVID-19 period. The analysis also showed that imported oil prices had a noticeable impact on inflation, particularly in the Transport & Communication category. Overall, the study highlights the influence of food prices, global oil prices, and economic disruptions on India's inflation trends.
